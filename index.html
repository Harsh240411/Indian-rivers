Himalayan River Systems

1. The Holy Ganga National River

Origin: Gangotri Glacier, Uttarakhand.

Course: Flows through Uttarakhand, UP, Bihar, and West Bengal before entering the Bay of Bengal.

Fact: It is 2,525 km long and forms the world's largest delta, the Sundarbans.

2. The Indus (Sindhu)

Origin: Near Mansarovar Lake, Tibet.

Indian Course: Enters India through Ladakh. It is the westernmost Himalayan river.

Tributaries: Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, and Satluj (The Punjab Plains).

Length: 2,900 km

3. The Brahmaputra

Origin: Chemayungdung Glacier, Tibet.

Indian Course: Enters India via Arunachal Pradesh and flows through Assam.

Fact: Known as Tsangpo in Tibet. It creates Majuli, the largest river island in the world.

4. The Yamuna

Origin: Yamunotri Glacier, Uttarakhand.

Course: It flows parallel to the Ganga and joins it at the Triveni Sangam in Prayagraj.

Peninsular River Systems

5. Narmada & Tapi

Narmada: Originates in Amarkantak Hills, MP. It flows in a Rift Valley between the Vindhya and Satpura ranges.

Tapi: Originates in Satpura ranges, MP. Both flow West into the Arabian Sea.

6. Godavari (Dakshin Ganga)

Origin: Trimbakeshwar, Maharashtra.

Fact: It is the longest Peninsular river, often called the Gautami or Old Ganga.

Length: 1,500 km

7. Mahanadi & Krishna

Mahanadi(860 km): Flows through Chhattisgarh and Odisha into the Bay of Bengal. Home to the Hirakud Dam.

Krishna(1,400 km): Originates near Mahabaleshwar. It is the second-largest east-flowing peninsular river.

8. Kaveri

Origin: Brahmagiri Hills, Karnataka.

Fact: It flows through Karnataka and Tamil Nadu, supporting massive agricultural belts.

Length: 760 km
